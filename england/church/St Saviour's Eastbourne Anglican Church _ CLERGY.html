The Revd
Nick MacNeill BTh

 

Fr Nick MacNeill, married to Dominique, originates from Brighton. After living in France for three years and working for a period of time in the tourism industry, he was ordained in 1993 and served in the dioceses of Exeter and St Albans. Owing to injury,
Fr. Nick was unable to continue in full-time ministry and has been part of the clergy team at St. Saviour's since 2006.


Fr Nick watches local football, enjoys reading, especially history, loves music, listens to the radio and with Dominique, likes exploring the beautiful county of Sussex.

Canon
Charles Lansdale BA


Fr Charles was born in 1938. Earliest memories include the Blitz in Plymouth where his mother was killed and where his father was an officer in the Royal Marines. Before going to University, He did a year’s teaching in a church secondary school then took a degree in anthropology and philosophy at Leeds University in the Hostel of the Resurrection before going on to the College of the Resurrection Mirfield. Ordained in 1961, he started off as a curate in Peckham, south London, for 4 years. He then joined the Mirfield Old Students Mission at Usuthu in the Diocese Zululand and Swaziland. Back to the UK in 1973 to be Vicar of All Saints, Benhilton in Sutton.

 

He then went on to a Team Ministry in Catford as Team Rector where he met and married Linda, who works in publishing. Then to a Team Ministry in Brighton in 1987 and finally ended up as Vicar of Eastbourne Parish Church in Old Town from 1997 to 2008 when he hit the buffers and had to retire!

The Revd
Christopher Hadfield BA


Christopher Hadfield was born in Manchester in 1939. He attended first Bolton School and later Rochdale Grammar School, and went up to Jesus College Cambridge in 1958 to read Classics. After graduating in 1961, he stayed on for a further year to read Theology, completing his training for the ministry at Wells Theological College. He was made deacon in 1965, serving his title in the parish of Wigton with Waverton in the Diocese of Carlisle. From 1970 till 2011 he taught at Newlands School Seaford, and holds the Bishop's Licence for the Diocese of Chichester.

 

He has been an honorary assistant priest at
St Saviour’s since December 1996, and also takes regular mid-week services in Seaford Parish Church. Fr Christopher is married to Elizabeth, whom he met at Newlands.

Father
John Wright BSc Cert Ed


Fr. John was born in Leicester in 1942, gained a Science Degree and an Educational Qualification at Birmingham University, taught for a year and then went to Theological College at St. Stephen’s House. Ordained in 1969 he served at St. Mary’s, Swanley Kent, then at St. Andrew’s Stockwell and St.John’s Leigh.For 30 years he was first Priest in Charge,
then Vicar of St. Cuthman’s, Whitehawk Estate, Brighton. He helped to run Renewal Conferences in Walsingham and High Leigh, and Days of Renewal
in London.

 

He was Chaplain of the Brighton Chapter of the Guild of the Servants of the Sanctuary. He is married to Brenda and they have two grown up sons. Fr. John collects playing cards and he enjoys cooking, especially Indian dishes.

Canon
Robert Fayers SSC

 

Fr. Robert hails from the Norfolk town of King's Lynn.  There he was a fourth-generation funeral director with his family's firm, until going to St. Stephen's House, Oxford, to be formed for priestly ministry.   

 

He served his 'title' under the legendary Fr. Diamond in the south-east London inner-city parish of St. Paul, Deptford.  He then moved to become incumbent of

St. Michael's, Beckenham.  After 12 years there he moved to Brighton, becoming Parish Priest of

St. Michael's and, later, also S. Paul's.  Fr Robert served in that city for 14 years, retiring late in 2014.  His interests include travel, visiting historic buildings and art galleries, and cooking. 

 

He is the Diocesan representative for the Anglican Centre in Rome.

 

 

Fr David Weaver SSC
BA (Wales) MA (Wales)
MA (London) Cert.Ed.(London)

 

Fr David was Vicar of St Richard's, Haywards Heath, and has served at All Saints, Hove and St Barnabas, Hove. He was ordained after a career in lecturing, management development and consultancy.

Fr David serves on the Diocesan Mission Fund and is a programme co-ordinator on the Initial Ministerial Education(IME) programme for new clergy in Chichester Diocese. Fr David is married to Lesley, who is responsible for Safeguarding training in the Diocese of Canterbury. Fr David and Lesley enjoy long walks with Taegan, their black Labrador.

Fr. Tony Fiddian-Green MA.Cert.Ed

 

Fr Tony was born in Warwickshire in1939. Birmingham, London and Sussex universities. Ordination/curacy: 1964 Bath and Wells. He has had various teaching appointments including two school chaplaincies and two headships – KS Bruton, Bath, Switzerland, Devon, RGS Worcester and Sussex. Commissioned in the RNR.  He then spent fourteen years as half-time parish priest, in Groombridge; the other half as a schools inspector for OFSTED and for the CofE.

 

Five years as Spirituality advisor with our Diocesan Mission & Renewal team, Chichester. He retired in 2011 to North Harbour, Eastbourne and in March 2016 moved to Devonshire Place in town.

Revd Dr David Musson M.Phil

 

Fr David was born in Lincolnshire and educated at the King's School, Grantham and at Lincoln Theological College. He graduated in psychology with the Open University and studied for  Masters and Doctorate degrees at the University of Wales, researching the personality of clergy and its implications in selection, appraisal and development of ministry.

​

He spent his full-time ministry in the diocese of Lincoln, in parishes and as chaplain of a large psychiatric hospital.

​

He is married to Katherine whom he met at a PCC meeting while they were both students!

Fr David enjoys photography, genealogy and cooking and tries to use his psychological skills in battling against an addiction to gadgets!

Fr Christopher Yates SSC
​

 

Fr Christopher is originally from Manchester, but he moved to Oxford at the age of eighteen where he met his wife Rachel and became a Policeman.

 

Anglo-Catholicism and ‘Oxford-Movement’ is something Fr Chris discovered while in Oxford. He trained for the priesthood at Cuddesdon, and St Stephen’s House where he was ordained Deacon in 2010.  He and his family moved to Newcastle, Australia and took up the post of Asst Priest at Williamtown, Medowie and Mallabula. He was ordained priest on 2nd May 2011 and installed as the parish priest for Raymond Terrace in October 2012.

Fr Chris's passions are music, liturgy, Theology (particularly Church history), and Manchester United! He is also passionate about working towards unity between Anglicans and Roman Catholics and is a priest of the Society of the Holy Cross (SSC).


He enjoys football (soccer), fishing, visiting craft breweries, and spending time with his family.
